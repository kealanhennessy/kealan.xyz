My athlete experience: Division I Rowing

The men's rowing team at the University of California, Berkeley, has consistently held a top 3 spot in the nation since its founding in the late 19th century. To maintain its status as one of the oldest and best collegiate rowing teams in the world, the training regime must be equally intense as it is rewarding; it means training at a minimum of 25 hours a week, beginning at 6am M-Sat, and afternoon practices at least 3 times a week.
At such a level I was very fortunate to have trained and competed alongside current and future Olympians, and to be coached by several former Olympians.
In terms of personal achievements, these experiences coalesced into a PAC-12 Championship win in 2018, and a silver medal at the Intercollegiate Rowing Association (IRA)
National Championsip in 2019.
